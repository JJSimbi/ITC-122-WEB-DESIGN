Online Gaming Towards Academic Performance

Created by Simbillo, Justine Jericho M.


Many people consider and insinuate that online gaming could be one reason for consuming children's
intellectual critical thinking and logical reasoning, particularly students at institutions. However,
is that the case at best in terms of confirmations? Are those anticipations optimistic and genuine?
One particular attribute that provides management on academic performance from online games is connecting
the courses between academic learning and game knowledge as a support system for students in their
daily lives employed at the educational institution. In terms of associations between the two,
online gaming is operated as a communication tool, where students accumulate communicational skills
and develop coordination with other people from playing the games, such as operating a "microphone"
to transmit responsiveness or utilizing a "chat box system" to classify and disseminate the messages.
However, regardless of the negative side of the scope, devotion to online games comprises positive
attributes that will provide advantages for academic performance.



Within those prospects, online gaming enriches one's cognitive mindset, enhancing and fostering
problem-solving, critical thinking, and decision-making as vital areas of development and growth of
the students. For instance, online games tend to maintain not just entertainment but also the awareness
of the cognitive skills they supply. They contain obstacles or hindrances that may require analytical
skills and spatial awareness for students to apprehend and exploit, especially in more educational-themed
games. Furthermore, the dynamic challenges illustrated in such online games encourage students to
adapt to more strategic approaches instead of being autonomous to only one. These experiences not only
enable players to persist from the complications but also nurture resiliency and a growth-oriented mindset.
In proliferation, multiplayer and collaborative online games facilitate communication, team cooperation,
and leadership aptitudes, as partakers must coordinate and strategize with others to achieve shared goals
or common objectives. These segments construct online gaming; besides being a source of engagement and
fun interactions, it is extravagantly reasonable for an effective and practical mechanism for cognitive
and personal development that can translate into academic performances, notably exactness, logical,
and natural deductive reasoning.



In summary, online games deliver significant benefits that enhance students' scholastic performance
and sustain their academic focus in school. Society, in general, should reconsider the potential
of online gaming as an impactful tool for developing academic skills and not solely for entertainment
value, as online gaming evolves students' cognitive abilities by enhancing skills such as problem-solving,
critical thinking, and decision-making. It also fosters collaboration, teamwork, and leadership, proving
to be a beneficial tool, for both academic and personal growth. With that said and done, the community
should consider undertaking more possibilities, such as online gaming, to develop research to apprehend
that regardless of certain disadvantages, there are possibilities that advantages offer.
